What is Stress?

Learn about the symptoms, causes, and healthy coping strategies

Understanding Stress

Stress is the bodyâ€™s natural response to challenges or demands. While a certain level of stress can motivate us, chronic stress can harm both physical and mental health if left unmanaged.

Symptoms of Stress

Stress can show up in different ways:

  • Headaches, muscle tension, or fatigue
  • Difficulty sleeping or insomnia
  • Rapid heartbeat or upset stomach
  • Feeling overwhelmed, irritable, or anxious
  • Trouble concentrating or making decisions
  • Changes in appetite or eating habits
  • Social withdrawal

Causes of Stress

Common triggers of stress include:

  • Work pressure, deadlines, or job insecurity
  • Financial difficulties
  • Relationship challenges
  • Major life changes or uncertainty
  • Health problems or caregiving responsibilities

Types of Stress

  • Acute Stress: Short-term stress caused by immediate challenges.
  • Chronic Stress: Long-lasting stress from ongoing problems.
  • Episodic Acute Stress: Frequent episodes of acute stress.

Coping Strategies & Support

While stress is part of life, healthy coping strategies can reduce its impact:

  • Relaxation Techniques: Deep breathing, meditation, or yoga.
  • Physical Activity: Regular exercise to release tension.
  • Healthy Lifestyle: Balanced diet, proper rest, and limiting caffeine/alcohol.
  • Time Management: Prioritizing tasks and taking breaks.
  • Social Support: Talking with friends, family, or counselors.
Important: If stress feels overwhelming or begins to affect your daily life, reach out to a healthcare professional for support.

Understanding stress and learning coping techniques are key to maintaining balance and overall well-being.
